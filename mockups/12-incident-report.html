Severity: Minor — cosmetic only, no injury, no third-party involvement.

What Happened (technician narrative)

While removing the failed battery, a small amount of acid had pooled in the bottom of the battery tray (case had a hairline crack on the underside that was not visible from above during the Vehicle Condition Report). When the battery was lifted clear, two drops of acid contacted the painted inner fender, leaving a faint discoloration approximately 1 cm in diameter.

I immediately neutralized the affected area with a baking-soda solution from the truck’s spill kit and rinsed with water. Photographed the affected area before and after neutralization. Notified the customer immediately, showed them the area, and explained what occurred. Customer accepted the neutralization and declined further mitigation.

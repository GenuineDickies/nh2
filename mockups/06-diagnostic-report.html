Findings

Two faults present:

  1. Battery is at end of life. Voltage at rest is below spec and load test fails. The battery cannot reliably crank the engine and is causing the no-start symptom.
  2. Alternator is failing. Charging-system output is well below spec. Even with a new battery, the system will not maintain charge and the no-start symptom will return within days to weeks.

Belt, ground straps, and cables are within spec. No other charging-system faults observed.

Recommendation

Replace both the battery and the alternator. Replacing only the battery will appear to resolve the symptom temporarily but the alternator will deplete the new battery within a short period. Replacing both items in a single visit is the most cost-effective and time-efficient path.

Both jobs are within the in-scope Mobile Mechanic catalog (Doc §3.6.1) and can be performed on-scene during a single visit. Required parts are on truck stock today.

White Knight — workbook mockups

All documents from the Operations Document Book, styled to match the reference Estimate (Times New Roman, table-heavy layout, black banner header). All data is fictional. The logo is a placeholder shield with "WK" — replace with the real logo when ready.

Customer-facing (signed)

These four are the documents the customer interacts with directly. Per Doc Principle 1, only the Waiver and Receipt require a customer signature for normal jobs; the other two only appear in special situations.

  1. Estimate — quote produced when an SR is approved for dispatch (Doc §1.3 doc 2)
  2. Service Authorization, Charges & Liability Waiver — signed before work begins (Doc §6)
  3. Change Order — signed when scope/price exceeds the Oregon threshold mid-job (Doc §1.8 P4)
  4. Customer Receipt — signed at completion; the liability anchor (Doc §11)

Customer-facing (cancellation paths)

  1. Customer Refusal of Service — customer declines after tech arrival; dispatch fee may apply (Doc §10.2)
  2. Technician Decline of Service — tech declines for safety / scope; full refund (Doc §10.3)

Customer-facing (informational)

  1. Diagnostic Report — Mobile Mechanic findings + estimate to repair (Doc §3.6.3)
  2. Tow Referral — info-provided log; customer signs nothing (Doc §12)

Internal / tech-completed

The customer doesn't sign these, but they're part of the job record and use the same visual language for company branding consistency.

  1. Service Request — intake form; system-of-record entry that initiates the job (Doc §5)
  2. Vehicle Condition Report — on-scene panel-by-panel inspection (Doc §7)
  3. Hazard Assessment — pre-work safety checklist + decision (Doc §8)
  4. Service Log — stage timeline, work narrative, measurements, parts (Doc §9)
  5. Incident Report — damage / injury / near-miss (Doc §13)
  6. Daily Truck Inspection — pre-shift; truck/equipment/M&B kit/Mobile Mechanic kit (Doc §14)
  7. End-of-Shift Reconciliation — cash, card, mileage, equipment close-out (Doc §15)
Print to PDF from any of these to get a paper-perfect mockup: Ctrl+P → Save as PDF → Letter, default margins. The CSS includes @page rules so screen and print look the same.
